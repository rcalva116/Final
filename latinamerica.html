Latin American Lore

El Silbón

El Silbon is a Venezuelan tale of a vicious whistling spirit. The Whistler is a terrifying man that haunts the Venezuelan and Colombian Llanos region. According to the legend, this man is a ghost who killed his father and later ate his organs. This tall man, who often is reported to wear a raggedy hat, is forever doomed to walk the region as a lost soul. He carries a bag filled with his father's bones. The Whistler gets his name from the bone-chilling whistling sound he emits. The scary part is that when the whistling sounds far away, El Silbón is actually nearby. It is said that hearing his whistle is a bad omen and signifies the death of the person.

La Ciguapa

La Ciguapa is a mythological creature who is described as a strange, wild woman living in the high mountains of the Dominican Republic. She is said to have magical powers and enchanting charm. This beautiful woman has beautiful long hair that wraps her naked body. The feet of La Ciguapa face backwards in order to trick her victims. The feet make it hard to see which way the creature is actually moving. Legend states that eye contact should be completely avoided. Those who look into her eyes will be enchanted by her powers, rendering them completely helpless.

Duendes

Folktale tells the story of spooky dwarf creatures that roam homes at night. In Spanish, duende originated as a contraction of the phrase dueño de casa, effectively "master of the house". They live inside the walls of homes, especially in the bedroom walls of young children. While seemingly inoffensive, duendes are often found frightening eople and playing tricks that usually get out of hand. Additionally, duendes are often reported to scare away people from their homes in order to protect hidden treasures.

El Sombreron

El sombreron is a spirit, or demon, who stalks and preys on young woman. All agree that he’s very small, dressed in all-black, and wears a big shiny belt, a pair of heavy boots and a large hat that hides his face. El Sombrerón seduces young women, wooing them with his sweet voice and beautiful melodies. Once they become bewitched, he stalks them and braids their hair. Under his spell, they become unable to eat or sleep. Eventually, until they die.

El Chupacabras

El Chupacabras, or Goat-sucker is a dog-like blood sucker creature who has been seen from throughout South and even North America. It was first reported in Puerto Rico, but it is mostly associated with Mexico and American border-states. The creature has never attacked people, yet it feeds off of live-stock. In fact, it gets its name by the style in which he feeds. The chupacabras primarily attacks goats, cows, and chickens, but numerous other farm animals have been reportedly killed as well. These animals are always exsanguinated, and the bodies are left behind, revealing two distinct puncture wounds.

El Coco

The Latino version of the bogeyman, El Cucuy, has terrified children for generations. This tale is used by parents across the Latin world to scare children into behaving. El Coco, or Cucuy as it is also known, is a shape-shifting figure that hides in closets or under beds, scares, and eventually eats kids who don’t listen to their parents.

La Llorona

La Llorona is the spirit of a weeping woman who cries out for her children. Some countries even refer to her as La Sayona. This is the most famous Latin American legend of all time. La Llorona is said to have been a beautiful woman who brutally killed her kids to be with the man she loved. When he rejected her, she killed herself. Now, the woman is doomed to wander, crying constantly, and vainly searching for her children for all of eternity. According to legend, she’ll kidnap wandering kids who roam the streets at night. All across Latin America, people report hearing her skin-crawling cry in the middle of the night. Thousands of sightings have also been reported from all over.

La Patasola

La Patasola is a one legged creature from Colombia. It makes its first appearance as a beautiful and seductive woman. It is even said she shifts into the appearance of her victims’ loved ones. She appears into anything or anyone in order to lure these people away from their groups. She tricks them into the jungles, where she reveals her true appearance as the one-legged ferocious vampire. The creature has a thirst for human flesh and blood. It attacks and eats the flesh and sucks the blood dry out its victims. Ironically, she protects nature and wild animals. Only attacking humans who seem to be harming the natural world.

La Luz Mala

La Luz Mala is a myticlights that are among the most famous legends in Argentine folklore. Some people claim that the unexplained flashes of light remain still in the horizon.However, there are others who say that the lights chased them at high speeds. The unexplained phenomenon is widely known, but many believe the lights are the spirits of the dead who didn’t get a proper Christian burial when they died.
