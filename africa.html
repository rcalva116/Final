African Lore

Grootslang

The Grootslang is a snake-elephant hybrid creature. Grootslang is an Afrikaans word meaning "great snake." The huge elephant-sized serpent dwells in a cave called the Wonder Hole in the Richtersveld area of South Africa. The story is that the original Grootslang held great power, so the African gods controlled the animal by splitting it into two species: the elephant and the snake. A Grootslang or two escaped and believed to have reproduced. The monster can grow up to 60 feet long. It is rumored that this creature lives in a cave is full of diamondsguards, yet no one truly knows because the it gaurds it well. The Grootslang is also said to live in warm rivers and lakes.

Inkanyamba

The Inkanyamba is a carnivorous eel-like animal from South Africa. From the legends of the Zulu and Xhosa people. It is believed that Inkanyambas can control the weather. They are said to have fins and flippers, and can grow to enormous size. Wile there are freshwater eels in South Africa that grow to around six feet long, they are far less scarier than described by the stories of the Inkanyamba. Additionally, the Inkanyamba is said to be living in a waterfall lake area in the northern forests near Pietermaritzburg, South Africa. The creature is most active in the summer months, it is believed that the Inkanyamba's anger causes the seasonal storms.

Kongamato

A flying monster called Kongamato is a flying reptile who may be a pterosaur. It has been spotted in in Zambia, Angola, and Congo. This cryptid lives in rivers and swamps and has a huge wingspan, yet it has no feathers. It is described as a reddish lizard with membranous wings and teeth in its beak. Its wingspan ranges from 4 to 100 feet across. A similar creature goes by other names in other parts of Africa. For example, there are accounts told of these creatures similar to the ones that describe the ropen of Papua New Guinea. The creature’s name means 'breaker of boats' because they are said to have attacked the canoes of people who ventured too close to the nearby rivers. Often, people were reported to have received wounds from the kongamatos. It is thought to eat mostly fish, but occasionally humans. Kongamato is also said to dig graves and steal human corpses.

Impundulu

The Impundulu, or as it is often referred, the Lightning Bird is a supernatural bird from South Africa. Its origin comes from Pondo, Zulu, and Xhosa folklore. The bird creature is as big as a human and can summon lightning and storms. It takes the form of a black and white bird It is reported that the creature is a shape shifter that can appear as a human. Sometimes it takes the form of a beautiful young man who seduces women. Additionally, it is said to be a supernatural familiar that guards a witch. It attacks people and drinks their blood. However, it also told that parts of the Impundulu or its eggs have medicinal powers.

Adze

The Adze is a vampire in the legends of the Ewe people of Ghana and Togo. It takes the form of a firefly, but if capture, it can take a human appearance. The vampire creature is dangerous in its human form. Th adze attacks and eats human organs, however it can be defeated. The adze, in its insect form, the adze sucks blood while its victim sleeps. Its preferred victims are young children. The victim becomes possessed by the adze’s spirit. People possessed by an adze are turned into witches, and are negatively influenced by the adze. A person can be suspected of being possessed for many reasons; women with brothers and are far better liked, old people when the young suddenly started dying, and the poor who envy the rich. Unfortunately there are victims who could fall sick and die. There is no defense against an adze.

Bili Ape

The Bili Ape is said to be an unknown ape species in Africa. Many of these cryptids are described as being a cross between a chimpanzee and a gorilla. A ghost ape fitting this description is called Ufiti in Malawi. In other areas it became known as the Bondo Mystery Ape. Reports led researchers to believe it was a large chimpanzee species that behaved more like gorillas. Various expeditions tried to find this species in Central Africa since skulls were found near the village of Bili in 1908. Yet, the creatures still remains a mystery because there have been no solid sightings. Scientist can not conclude it is a real animal, thus its presence remains a tall tale.

Gbahali

The Gbahali is a crocodile-like reptilian creature from Liberia. It said to grow up to 20 to 30 feet long. Its snout is shorter than a crocodiles, yet its has longer legs. Its not considered a legend among Liberian hunters. They report that it is a real creature they have caught and eaten. Some witnesses have been shown a picture of theextinct animal Postosuchus, and some recognized it as the Gbahali. The Gbahali reportedly kills people who stray into the river. The Postosuchus died out over 200 million years ago and lived in the United States, not Liberia. The Gbahali could be a different undiscovered crocodillian creature.

Ninki Nanka

A Ninki Nanka is a legendary creature in West African folklore. Legends state that a Ninki Nankara lives in the Gambia River. It is a dragon-like creature with the body of a crocodile, the head of a horse, with horns, and a long neck. It is said to grow from 30 to 50 feet long. Those who have claimed to have had an encounter with a Ninki Nanka claim it looks similar to a Chinese dragon. This creature is reptilian and possibly dragon-like, and is a swamp creature. The animal is said to be extremely large and very dangerous. Reportedly, children who disobey their parents and go into the swamp get taken by the Ninki Nanka. The story of the Ninki Nanka is oral and has spread from tribe to tribe all over Africa.

Popobawa

The Popobawa has been reported in Zanzibar and Tanzania. The creature is a demon who appears as a normal human by day, and a one-eyed, bat-winged monster at night. The Popobawa attacks in the dark, and is particularly vicious to those who don't believe in him. Attacks were first reported in 1965 on the island of Pemba. The name Popobawa in Swahili translates to "bat wing." A popular origin story of Popobawa states angry sheikh released a Jinn to take vengeance on his neighbors. The sheikh lost control of the jinn, who in turn turned into a demon creature.

Mokèlé-mbèmbé

The Mokèlé-mbèmbé is a cryptid that looks like a dinosaur. This dino-like creature is from the Congo River region. The name means "one who stops the flow of rivers", an indication of its size. Moreover, this creature is sometimes described as a living creature, or sometimes as a spirit. Those that heard or that allegedly saw the entity describe it as a large quadrupedal herbivore with a smooth skin, a long neck and a single tooth, sometimes said to be a horn. Numerous expeditions have taken place with the aim of finding this huge creature. They have all come back with reports of vague sightings and mysterious footprints.

Tikoloshe

A Tikoloshe is an evil-spirited gremlin in Zulu mythology. Tikoloshes are described as short hairy human-like figures that can turn invisible by swallowing a pebble. Tikoloshe is a dwarf-like water sprite. It is believed that a shaman sends a Tikoloshe to hex enemies, causing anything from fright, to illness, or even death. At its least harmful, it is often a tokoloshe can be used to scare children. The creature might be banished by a pastor, who has the power to expel it from the area.
